Data Protection

Privacy Policy

We take the protection of your personal data seriously. This policy explains in full what data we collect, why, how we use it, and your rights under applicable law.

Last updated: 1 June 2026  ·  GDPR / DSGVO compliant  ·  Version 1.0

Section 01

Data Controller

The controller responsible for the processing of personal data collected through this website and in the course of our business activities is:

Controller Details (Art. 13 para. 1 lit. a GDPR)

Legal name Chalor Capital Allocation & Institutional Group
Registered address Berlin, Germany
Email (general) chalorgroup@outlook.fr

References to "we", "us", or "Chalor" in this policy refer to Chalor Capital Allocation & Institutional Group as the data controller. Where we act as a data processor on behalf of a client, a separate data processing agreement (DPA) governs that relationship.

Section 02

Scope & Legal Basis of Processing

This Privacy Policy applies to all personal data processed by Chalor Capital Allocation & Institutional Group in connection with:

  • Visits to and use of our website at www.chalor.com and any related subdomains;
  • Enquiries submitted via our contact form or sent by email;
  • Business correspondence and client relationship management;
  • The performance of capital allocation, advisory, and institutional services for our clients;
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory obligations applicable to financial services providers.

We process personal data only where a valid legal basis under Article 6 of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR) applies. The primary legal bases we rely upon are:

Legal Basis GDPR Reference When Applied
Consent Art. 6(1)(a) Non-essential cookies, marketing communications
Contract performance Art. 6(1)(b) Processing necessary to deliver agreed services to clients
Legal obligation Art. 6(1)(c) KYC/AML checks, regulatory reporting, tax records
Legitimate interests Art. 6(1)(f) Website security, fraud prevention, business communications, responding to enquiries

Where we process special categories of personal data (Art. 9 GDPR), we will explicitly identify the applicable basis (typically explicit consent or a statutory requirement).

Section 03

Website Data Collection

Server Log Files

When you access our website, our web server automatically records certain technical information in server log files. This data is collected for the purpose of ensuring the stable and secure operation of the website and does not constitute profiling of individual visitors. The following data is recorded:

  • IP address of the requesting device (anonymised or in full, depending on configuration);
  • Date and time of the request;
  • URL of the page accessed;
  • HTTP status code and volume of data transferred;
  • Referring URL (the page from which the request originated, if applicable);
  • Browser type and version, operating system, and device type.

Server log data is retained for a maximum of 14 days for security and technical diagnostic purposes and is then automatically deleted, unless longer retention is required due to a specific security incident. The legal basis for this processing is our legitimate interest in operating a secure and functional website (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

Analytics

We may use privacy-preserving analytics tools to understand aggregate patterns of website usage (e.g., most visited pages, geographic distribution of visitors, device types). Where such tools are used, they are configured to anonymise or pseudonymise data prior to processing and do not create individual behavioural profiles. Where analytics tools that process personal data are activated, they are subject to your consent via our cookie banner. Please refer to Section 7 (Cookies & Tracking) for full details.

No Automated Decision-Making

We do not employ automated decision-making or profiling based on data collected through the website that produces legal effects or similarly significant impacts on visitors (Art. 22 GDPR).

Section 04

Contact Form & Enquiries

When you submit an enquiry through our website contact form, we collect the personal data you provide. Depending on the fields you complete, this may include:

  • Full name;
  • Company or organisation name;
  • Email address;
  • Enquiry type (mandate category selected from drop-down);
  • The content of your message.

We process this data for the purpose of handling your enquiry, assessing the feasibility of a potential mandate relationship, and responding to you. The legal basis is our legitimate interest in responding to incoming business communications (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR), supplemented by pre-contractual steps at your request (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR) where applicable.

Please note: We will never use the information you submit via the contact form to add you to marketing distribution lists or share it with third parties for marketing purposes without your explicit consent. If your enquiry leads to a formal mandate engagement, your data will be processed under the terms of our client agreement and this Privacy Policy.

Enquiry data is retained for 36 months from the date of receipt (or longer if a client relationship is established), after which it is securely deleted unless we have a legal obligation to retain it for a longer period.

Section 05

Email Correspondence

When you contact us by email, or when we conduct business correspondence with you by email, we process personal data contained in those communications. This section explains in detail how we handle email-based personal data.

What We Collect from Email Communications

Email correspondence may contain, among other things:

  • Sender information: Your name, email address, and any further contact details included in your email signature or message body;
  • Communication content: The subject line, body text, and any files or attachments you choose to include;
  • Metadata: Technical transmission data including IP addresses of mail servers, timestamps, message identifiers, and routing information generated by email protocols (SMTP, IMAP/POP3);
  • Reply-chain history: Prior messages in a conversation thread that are included in subsequent replies.

Purpose and Legal Basis for Email Processing

We process personal data contained in email communications for the following purposes, each with its applicable legal basis:

Purpose Legal Basis (GDPR)
Responding to your enquiry or message Art. 6(1)(f) — Legitimate interest; or Art. 6(1)(b) — Contract performance
Assessing and onboarding potential clients Art. 6(1)(b) — Pre-contractual steps at your request
Maintaining records of business correspondence Art. 6(1)(f) — Legitimate interest in accurate record-keeping
Compliance with regulatory obligations (e.g. MiFID II communication archiving) Art. 6(1)(c) — Legal obligation
Fraud prevention and security Art. 6(1)(f) — Legitimate interest

Email Service Provider & Infrastructure

Our email infrastructure is operated using professional business email services. Email data may be processed on servers located within the European Economic Area (EEA). Where our email provider stores or processes data outside the EEA, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with Chapter V of the GDPR (see Section 9, International Transfers).

Regulatory Archiving of Business Email

As a financial services provider subject to regulation under BaFin, AMF, and FINMA frameworks, we are subject to legal obligations to archive business-related electronic communications, including email correspondence relating to investment advice, portfolio management, order execution, and other regulated activities. This obligation arises under MiFID II (Article 16(6)–(7)) and applicable national implementing legislation.

Emails relating to regulated activities are archived for a minimum of 5 years (or up to 7 years where required by the competent authority) from the date of the communication, regardless of whether a mandate relationship was subsequently established. The legal basis for this archiving is our legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR).

Confidentiality of Email Content

All email communications received by Chalor are treated as confidential business correspondence. Access to mailboxes and archived emails is restricted to authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis and is governed by our internal information security policies.

Important security notice: Email is not inherently a secure method of transmitting highly sensitive financial or personal information. We recommend that particularly sensitive information — such as account details, identity documents, or confidential investment mandates — be exchanged through our secure client portal (where available) or by encrypted email. Please contact us at contact@chalor.com if you require guidance on secure document transmission.

Unsolicited Commercial Email

We do not send unsolicited commercial email (spam). If you receive an email purporting to be from Chalor that you did not request and did not consent to, please notify us immediately at chalorgroup@outlook.fr so that we may investigate.

Retention of General Email Correspondence

Non-regulated business email correspondence (i.e. general enquiries, administrative communications not covered by regulatory archiving requirements) is retained for a period of 36 months from the date of the last communication in a thread, after which it is securely deleted unless a legal obligation or legitimate interest requires longer retention. Please refer to Section 10 (Retention Periods) for full details.

Section 06

Client & Mandate Data

Where we enter into a formal mandate or advisory relationship with a client (whether an individual, family office, or corporate entity), we process personal and organisational data to the extent necessary to deliver our services and fulfil our regulatory obligations. This includes:

  • Identity and verification data (name, date of birth, nationality, passport or ID document details) collected as part of our Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) processes;
  • Contact and communication details;
  • Financial information, including investment objectives, risk tolerance, portfolio holdings, and transaction history, required under MiFID II suitability assessment rules;
  • Beneficial ownership information for corporate and institutional clients;
  • Source of wealth and source of funds information where required by applicable AML legislation;
  • Tax identification numbers (e.g. for CRS/FATCA reporting obligations);
  • Records of investment advice given, orders placed, and portfolio decisions made.

The legal bases for processing client data are: performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b)); compliance with legal obligations including AML, MiFID II, FATCA/CRS (Art. 6(1)(c)); and legitimate interests in providing high-quality financial services and maintaining accurate business records (Art. 6(1)(f)).

Clients who are natural persons are provided with a separate, detailed client-specific privacy notice at the point of onboarding, covering all categories of data processed in the context of their individual mandate. This website privacy policy supplements but does not replace that document.

Section 07

Cookies & Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies on our website. Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser when you visit our site. We distinguish between strictly necessary cookies and optional cookies requiring your consent.

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the basic functionality of the website (e.g. remembering your cookie consent preference). They do not require your consent and cannot be disabled without impairing core site functions. The legal basis is our legitimate interest in operating a functional website.

Optional Cookies (Consent Required)

With your consent, we may set analytics or functional cookies that help us understand how visitors interact with our website and improve user experience. You may grant or withdraw your consent at any time through our cookie banner or by adjusting your browser settings.

A full description of all cookies used, their purposes, providers, and retention periods is available in our separate Cookie Policy.

Your consent to non-essential cookies is stored locally in your browser (chalor_cookie_choice) and does not expire until cleared. You may withdraw consent at any time by clearing your browser's local storage or revisiting our cookie preference centre.

Section 08

Third Parties & Recipients of Personal Data

We do not sell personal data to third parties. We share personal data only in the following circumstances:

Service Providers (Data Processors)

We engage carefully selected third-party service providers who process personal data on our behalf, strictly under our instructions and pursuant to written data processing agreements (DPAs) in accordance with Art. 28 GDPR. These include:

  • IT infrastructure and cloud hosting providers;
  • Email infrastructure and archiving providers;
  • CRM and business communication platforms;
  • Cybersecurity and fraud prevention services;
  • Professional advisors (legal, tax, audit) subject to confidentiality obligations.

Regulatory & Supervisory Authorities

We may be required to disclose personal data to competent regulatory authorities (BaFin, AMF, FINMA, or other relevant supervisors), law enforcement agencies, or courts in connection with our legal and regulatory obligations. Such disclosures are made only to the extent legally required and in accordance with applicable data protection law.

Third-Party Integrations on the Website

Our website uses certain third-party resources that may result in technical data being transmitted to their servers:

  • Google Fonts: Loaded from Google's servers (fonts.googleapis.com, fonts.gstatic.com). Google may receive your IP address as part of this request. We have implemented resource loading in a manner designed to minimise data transfer where possible.
  • Tabler Icons CDN (jsdelivr.net): Icon font loaded from a CDN; may result in IP address transmission to CDN provider.
  • AOS Library CDN (cdnjs.cloudflare.com): Scroll animation library; processed by Cloudflare's CDN infrastructure.

The use of these third-party resources is based on our legitimate interest in providing a high-quality, performant website experience (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR).

Section 09

International Data Transfers

As a firm operating across Continental Europe and North America, some of our activities may involve the transfer of personal data to countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA). Transfers of personal data to third countries are only carried out where one of the following safeguards applies in accordance with Chapter V GDPR:

  • Adequacy decision: The European Commission has determined that the destination country provides an adequate level of data protection (e.g. Switzerland, which is relevant to our FINMA-regulated activities);
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs): We have implemented the European Commission's standard contractual clauses (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914) with recipients in third countries where an adequacy decision does not exist;
  • Binding Corporate Rules or other appropriate safeguards where applicable.

You may request further information about the specific safeguards in place for any particular transfer by contacting us at chalorgroup@outlook.fr.

Section 10

Retention Periods

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. The following table summarises our standard retention periods:

Data Category Retention Period Basis
Server log files 14 days Legitimate interest (security)
Contact form enquiries (no mandate) 36 months from last interaction Legitimate interest (business records)
General email correspondence 36 months from last message Legitimate interest (business records)
Regulated investment communications (MiFID II) 5 years (up to 7 years if required by competent authority) Legal obligation (MiFID II Art. 16)
Client KYC/AML records 5 years after end of business relationship Legal obligation (AMLD, GwG)
Client mandate & portfolio records 10 years after end of mandate Legal obligation / Legitimate interest
Accounting & tax records 10 years (Germany: § 257 HGB, § 147 AO) Legal obligation
Cookie consent records Duration of browser local storage; max. 24 months Legal obligation (demonstrating consent)

Following the applicable retention period, personal data is securely deleted or anonymised in a manner that prevents reconstruction of the original personal data.

Section 11

Your Data Subject Rights

As a data subject under the GDPR, you have the following rights in respect of your personal data processed by Chalor Capital Allocation & Institutional Group. We are committed to responding to all valid requests within one calendar month of receipt, extendable by a further two months in complex cases, with notification to you.

Right of Access (Art. 15)

You may request confirmation of whether we process your personal data and, if so, access to a copy of that data together with information about the processing.

Right to Rectification (Art. 16)

You may request correction of inaccurate personal data or completion of incomplete data held about you.

Right to Erasure (Art. 17)

You may request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn, or processing is unlawful — subject to legal retention obligations.

Right to Restriction (Art. 18)

You may request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances, such as while the accuracy of the data is being contested.

Right to Portability (Art. 20)

Where processing is based on consent or contract and carried out by automated means, you may receive your data in a structured, commonly used machine-readable format.

Right to Object (Art. 21)

You may object to processing based on legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)). We will cease processing unless compelling legitimate grounds override your interests.

Right Not to Be Subject to Automated Decisions (Art. 22)

You have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing that produce significant legal or similarly significant effects.

Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 7(3))

Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent prior to withdrawal.

To exercise any of your rights, please submit a request in writing to privacy@chalor.com. We may request proof of identity before processing your request in order to protect your personal data from unauthorised access.

Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe that our processing of your personal data infringes applicable data protection law, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the competent supervisory authority. In Germany, this is the data protection authority of the federal state in which we are registered. You may also contact the Federal Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (Bundesbeauftragter für den Datenschutz und die Informationsfreiheit, BfDI) at www.bfdi.bund.de.

Section 12

Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, or access, in accordance with Art. 32 GDPR. These measures include, but are not limited to:

  • Encrypted data transmission via TLS/SSL for all web traffic;
  • Encrypted storage of sensitive client data at rest;
  • Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorised personnel on a need-to-know basis;
  • Regular security reviews, vulnerability assessments, and penetration testing;
  • Multi-factor authentication for access to systems containing personal data;
  • Staff training and awareness programmes on data protection and information security;
  • Incident response procedures, including data breach notification protocols in compliance with Art. 33–34 GDPR.

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the competent supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach (Art. 33 GDPR) and, where required, will notify affected individuals without undue delay (Art. 34 GDPR).

Section 13

Children's Data

Our website and services are directed exclusively at adults and professional or institutional clients. We do not knowingly collect or process personal data of individuals under the age of 18. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data of a minor, we will take steps to delete such data promptly. If you believe that a minor has submitted personal data to us, please contact us at chalorgroup@outlook.fr.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our data processing practices, legal requirements, or regulatory guidance. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page and, where appropriate, notify you by email or by a prominent notice on our website.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our website or services following any such changes constitutes your acknowledgment of the revised policy. The version of this policy in effect at the time we receive your personal data applies to the processing of that data, subject to any mandatory updates required by law.

Archived versions of this policy are available on request by contacting chalorgroup@outlook.fr.
